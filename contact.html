Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior programming experience to join?

Not at all! We welcome students of all skill levels. We have beginner-friendly workshops and mentorship programs to help you get started. Many of our most successful members started with no programming experience.

What programming languages do you use?

We primarily use Python for machine learning projects, as it has the best ecosystem of ML libraries like PyTorch, TensorFlow, and scikit-learn. We also occasionally use JavaScript for web-based AI applications and R for data analysis.

How much time commitment is required?

Our weekly meetings are 1.5 hours every Friday. Additional time depends on your involvement level - you might spend 2-5 hours per week on projects, competitions, or self-study. We're flexible and understand academic priorities.

Can I join mid-semester?

Absolutely! We accept new members throughout the year. We'll help you catch up with current projects and pair you with a mentor to get you started. Just come to one of our Friday meetings or contact us via email.

What kind of projects do members work on?

Our projects range from beginner-friendly image classifiers to advanced research collaborations with Stanford. Recent projects include real-time object detection, sentiment analysis bots, game AI, and medical image analysis.

Do you participate in competitions?

Yes! We regularly participate in Kaggle competitions, Google AI challenges, and local hackathons. We've won several regional competitions and provide training and team support for members who want to compete.
